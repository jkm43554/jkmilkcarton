brand identity
mockingbird talent agency
brand identity design

details...

Mockingbird Talent Agency is an agency built by a creative for creatives. The music industry is filled with greed and misdeeds. The founder of MTA, Atticus Roness, knows the ins and outs of the industry. He knows what does and what doesn’t work, he knows how to cater to creatives because he is one, and he knows where booking agents fall short because he’s spent years working with them. MTA seeks to unveil an innovative brand of transparency, creativity, and trust.

The talent agency industry overall has a very professional and industrial look in it’s identity. I approached MTA’s identity to keep this professional feel but also bring out the creative and innovative side of MTA. Some ways this was achieved was through finding a professional type with creative alternate character and glyph options available, choosing a secondary color that would represent the creative aspects, and creating a brandmark which is rare to come by in the talent agent industry.

To learn of more ways I achieved this identity, take a look at the brand strategy guide.

album rollout
the corduroy blue
graphic design / illustration

details...

The Corduroy Blue (TCB) is a power pop band based in Atl, Ga. TCB aims to blend the charisma of 60s/70s rock-pop with the charm of 90s Brit-pop; all within a modern context.

TCB needed a full rollout of media and merchandise to promote their debut self-titled album and attract an audience to their shows. TCB has gained a cult following among the underground Atlanta rock scene for the atmosphere created at their shows, and the connections they make with their fans and other local bands. So it’s important that the designs appeal to new audiences while showing love to current fans and friends. My role was to design tour/show posters, t-shirts, stickers, and instagram posts for the rollout.

My solution was to capture the feelings and energy created at their live shows. A pushing invite to experience TCB’s shows would also end in a pushing invite to listen to their new album. Another benefit to this is that current fans would recognize the energy and presence they love and be compelled to experience more.

I set to capture their show presence (a mix of iconic and mystic yet approachable and inviting) through heroic cartoon like illustrations that match the tour name. The stickers also make funny references that longtime fans and members of the scene will know and enjoy allowing the designs to appeal both to a new and old audience.

The tour received a wonderful response with all sold-out shows. While the merchandise didn’t sell out, TCB sold many shirts with people wearing them right after buying them and during the shows. The stickers have not been ordered, but I am very excited to see the response they get once they’re laid out at shows.

social media graphics
daily grind marietta
graphic design / illustration / lettering

details...

The Daily Grind (DG) is a local and family owned coffeeshop in Marietta, Ga. DG needed a brand identity and graphics to keep their loyal customers updated. Before this update DG would only post pictures with info in the caption that most followers would ignore. They also didn’t have consistency and cohesion in their identity to represent the brand. I worked with the social media manager to establish a color palette, typographic identity, and brand identity.

Inspired by another local coffee shop I decided to use big display fonts to inform customers of updates and announcements, or compel them to read the caption. We decided upon a warm color grading for the photos and chose the color palette from that. The colors were also taken from photos of the store, which has a monochromatic look across the inside and outside of the store. At my suggestion each graphic had a background pattern of hand drawn illustrations related to coffee or the current holiday season of the time. DG vey much enjoyed this. I chose the hand drawn doodle style of illustration to convey the sense of family DG is known for in its community.

The designs resulted in less emails and phone calls to the shop for simple information such as updated store hours, closures, specials, etc.. We were even able to use the graphics to help spread word on finding a thief who stole a regular customer’s truck and trailer the regular uses for his landscaping company. Less time talking to customers on the phone and email resulted in more time to prep and get orders out quicker.

'waterfall'- single promo videos
big shanty
motion graphics

details...

Big Shanty is a bedroom-pop artist based in Atlanta, GA. He seeks to pursue and capture new and unfamiliar combinations of sounds. The ambiguity in this combination creates a mysterious identity that tastefully combines elements of the unknown with elements of what’s familiar. Big Shanty has a small following and needed to grow the reach of his social media presence to drive people to listen to his single “Waterfall”.

With a month before release, my role was to present graphics that would provide information of the release. I decided to use minimalist motion graphics to get the message out as (at the time) instagrams algorithm favored videos and motion. I also proposed to encourage his followers to pre-save the single, follow him on their select streaming service, and share the announcements as these actions are favored by both instagrams algorithm as well as streaming service algorithms. Taking advantage of this would extend his reach on social media, increase engagement, and potentially lead to first party playlist curations.

“Waterfall” creates a mysterious atmosphere sonically and lyrically. He aids this atmosphere with minimalist composition, production, and arrangement. Immediately the image of outer-space came to mind when brainstorming the designs of this song. I chose gradients flowing in a loop through neighboring hues to match the imagery of a waterfall. I set them in a dark background to match the outer-space like atmosphere represented in the song. For the typography I went through a lot of famous sci-fi media to find inspiration. I ended up with a slightly manipulated simple serif font as inspired by Neon Genesis Evangelion.

Unfortunately, the single was never placed on a first-party playlist, but it did end up being the most pre-saved single of Big Shanty’s audience and the most streamed song of his up to that point. The song was also placed on many of his followers playlists which sets him up for more successful releases in the future as Spotify and Apple Music's algorithm favors playlist placements.

gradient music festival
maryville university
graphic design

details...

Gradient is the result of a final exam school project from my time at Maryville University. Students were tasked to utilize Photoshop, Illustrator, and Indesign to create a brand identity, poster, booklet, and tickets for a fictional concert.

I decided to create a music festival called “Gradient”, drawing inspiration from a playlist I made earlier that semester. The playlist was a curation of artists I noticed made songs tackling tough and dark subjects, but always with a sense of hope and moving forward. The contrast between the light and dark within the same sonic and thematic setting inspired the name “Gradient”.

With the title “Gradient” it was pretty obvious which direction to take the design. Because of the “dark to light” theme I wanted the gradient used in the design to go from black to white with, at most, a slight hue added to both values, but extreme contrast must be presented. Most of the artists on the playlist (and therefore chosen for the project) have a somewhat minimalist sound so I decided to use a monochromatic scheme. I chose to stay within the blue hue to match the bluesy-ness of the artists represented. For the typography I went with one classic serif with different weights to compliment and really drive the minimalist theme. I feel the minimalism really amplifies the contrast and helps to push the “light surrounded by dark” feeling.

I took some risks with what the professor asked for to much of her approval. Instead of tickets I designed passes. For the booklet we needed to provide photos of each artist. I decided to cut out the headliners to create contrast. For the supporting acts I kept them in the photo to show they weren’t headliners. The project received high praise and a great response from the professor as well as the class.

tour poster
the corduroy blue
graphic design

details...

The Corduroy Blue (TCB) is a power pop band based in Atl, Ga. TCB aims to blend the charisma of 60s/70s rock-pop with the charm of 90s Brit-pop; all within a modern context. TCB needed a poster for their early winter tour. In addition the poster design would be used for social media as well.

My solution, once again, was to capitalize on the atmosphere created at their shows. TCB has contrasting energy and themes that really work well together. On one hand they portray a sense of flashiness and glamour somewhat reminiscent of disco and pop. On the other hand they have an aggressive recklessness and edge found in punk and underground rock.

To emphasize the flashiness and glamour I decided to make the band the focal point and up front on the visual hierarchy. I also used some blur to add a sense of shininess as well as some light coming from the bottom to represent search lights used for glamorous events such as Hollywood award ceremonies.

For the edginess and recklessness I used placement inspired by the cut and paste style of placement seen on concert posters through 60s and 70s UK and other underground proto punk cities.

real 2 reel Studios
graphic design

details...

Real 2 Reel(R2R) Studios is a music studio based in Atlanta, Ga. Owned by the founders son, Will Turpin(Collective Soul), they’ve hosted bands such as Kansas, Lynyrd Skynyrd, and Collective Soul.

R2R needed a poster and social media graphics to promote their Christmas concert. The concert was a showcase of the artists that recorded there throughout the year. Since their lineup consisted of gritty rock artists, they wanted to be cautious that the Christmas theme didn't give off a “cute” feeling. They wanted it to be “Christmassy” but stay “edgy and organic”.

Capitalizing on green being both an organic earthy color as well as a Christmassy color, I used it to start off the color palette. I illustrated a guitar and Christmas tree with some rough and simple textures overlayed. I drew inspiration from vintage style Christmas cards for the overall aesthetic. I used a stylized sans serif font paired with a vintage looking script font. To avoid a cute look I stayed away from cute illustrations and the use of a lot of red. R2R loved the poster and the showcase ended up being a sold out show.
